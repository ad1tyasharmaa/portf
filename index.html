About

I'm a final-year Mechanical Engineering student at NSUT Delhi with a second brain wired for software. That dual perspective shapes how I approach everything: I don't reach for a solution until I've mapped the problem — its structure, its failure modes, its constraints. Whether decomposing pollution time-series into trainable signals or designing a database schema that holds at scale, the process is the same: understand deeply, then build deliberately.

I've published three peer-reviewed papers — in IEEE DELCON and Springer — presenting work on AQI forecasting, glaucoma detection, and brain tumour classification. Each required not just technical rigour, but the ability to distil months of experimentation into arguments that hold up under expert scrutiny. Winning Best Research Paper at ICRTC-2025 was as much a communication win as a technical one.

On the product side, I co-founded Knell and led a team from zero to a live platform with 15K+ users and mobile apps on both stores. I'm comfortable with ambiguity, I stay calm when things break, and I believe good engineers communicate as precisely as they compute.

Research

①
Brain Tumor Classification via ML + Polar Lights Optimizer
Springer · Instrumentation Dept, NSUT
Extracted 38 features from brain MRI images using GLCM, GLRLM, and Hu Moments. Applied Z-score normalization and reduced to 25 features via the Polar Lights Optimizer. XGBoost outperformed all classifiers — achieving 96.20% accuracy after feature selection, with improved per-class precision, recall, and F1-score across four tumour classes.
②
Automated Glaucoma Detection via ML Classifiers
🏆 Best Paper · ICRTC-2025 · SRM × Springer
GLCM-based texture feature extraction followed by Relief-based feature selection. Evaluated SVM, Random Forest, and XGBoost classifiers — achieving 85.03% accuracy with XGBoost. Evaluated across precision, recall, and F1-score metrics.
③
Hybrid CEEMDAN-BiLSTM-XGBoost Framework for AQI Prediction
IEEE DELCON 2025 · Instrumentation Dept, NSUT
Decomposed AQI time-series signals using CEEMDAN, then applied BiLSTM and XGBoost models per component. Aggregated predictions achieve MAE 3.59 · RMSE 4.44 · R² 0.98 — outperforming standalone models and hybrid baselines by a significant margin.
